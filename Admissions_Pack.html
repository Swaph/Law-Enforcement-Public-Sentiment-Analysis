Academic/Entrepreneur Impact Record (Project Showcase)

This project tackles a difficult applied-NLP question: how to quantify public sentiment in multilingual, code-switched civic discourse where standard sentiment tools can underperform. My role was end-to-end, from data filtering and preprocessing to model training, evaluation, and deployment-ready packaging.

I designed the notebook pipeline to isolate Kenya-specific law-enforcement events, used multilingual transformer-assisted labeling for bootstrap sentiment signals, and then built a domain-adapted classical baseline (TF-IDF + Logistic Regression) with imbalance handling and hyperparameter tuning. I also shipped a reproducible package workflow with persisted artifacts and API-serving capability.

The impact is practical and policy-relevant: this system produces measurable sentiment indicators that can support transparent monitoring of public service outcomes. The work aligns with SDG 16 by improving evidence quality for institutional accountability and civic-trust analysis. References below verify source code, methodology, outputs, and documentation.
